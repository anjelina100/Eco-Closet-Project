About Us

Eco-Closet is a platform dedicated to advocating for sustainable fashion, raising awareness about the negative impact of fast fashion— both environmentally and socially, and helping people transition from buying fast fashion to making more sustainable purchases. The main feature of Eco-Closet, “Find Your Style Quiz,” is an assessment based on your current wardrobe preferences that helps you find clothing styles you should try. In addition, we also offer sustainable brand names with a style that most matches your quiz result.

We also have a “Style Moodboard” that offers visual representation of outfit/style ideas with persona labels underneath each image to better help you choose your preferred look. The board allows you to view hundreds of looks with the shuffle button. However, if you pin an image to the board, that image will stay, while all the other images will change into different ones. We also provide resources and news about the environmental impact of the fashion industry, and ways that we can contribute to the collective effort of protecting our planet.

Impact

What really is "fast fashion"? Ultimately, fast fashion is a design, manufacturing, and marketing method focused on rapidly producing high volumes of clothing in an uneconomical way. Fast fashion negatively impacts our Earth in plenty of significant ways. For instance, 2.8 million tons of clothing are sent to landfills in the US every year This effects not ony us, but our future children and grandchildren, as well as all the animals living on Earth. Now, there are plenty of animals going extinct due to the drastic changes of our deteriorating Earth, animals like polar bears, monarch butterflies, cheetahs, koalas, and the list keeps going. Global warming is projected to commit over one-third of the Earth's animal and plant species to extinction by 2050 if current greenhouse gas emissions trajectories continue. One of many ways YOU can help prevent this from happening, is by shopping at sustainable clothing brands, rather than fast fashion brands.

The fashion industry produces 10 percent of humanity’s carbon emissions

It takes about 700 gallons of water to produce one cotton shirt.

The high demands of producing inexpensive clothing are pushing brands to use cheap labor, low-quality fabrics, and provide unsafe working conditions.
